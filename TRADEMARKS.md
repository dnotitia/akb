# AKB Trademarks

"AKB", the AKB logo, "Dnotitia", and "Seahorse" are trademarks of
**Dnotitia, Inc.** The [PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0](./LICENSE) license
covers the *software* — it grants **no trademark rights**.

The license restricts what you can DO with the software.
The trademark restricts what you can CALL it.
These are separate legal layers — both apply.

## What this means in practice

### Permitted

- Factual references: "compatible with AKB", "built on AKB", "uses AKB
  for storage" — when accurate.
- Linking to the upstream project as "AKB" in documentation.
- Distributing unmodified copies under their original name within the
  noncommercial use grant of the license.

### Not permitted (without prior written permission from Dnotitia, Inc.)

- Distributing a fork, modified copy, or derivative work under the
  "AKB" name, the AKB logo, or any confusingly similar name (e.g.
  "AKB Pro", "Open AKB", "AKB+", "AKB-X", "MyAKB").
- Using the "AKB" name, logo, or any Dnotitia mark in a way that
  implies endorsement, partnership, certification, or origin from
  Dnotitia, Inc.
- Using the "Dnotitia" or "Seahorse" names or logos in derivative
  works, marketing, or product names.

### If you fork and modify

Rebrand your fork under a different name and logo before distributing
it. You may state factually that your fork is "based on AKB" or
"derived from AKB", but the fork itself must not be called "AKB" or
any confusingly similar variant.

## Why these rules exist

Trademarks protect users — when someone sees "AKB", they should know
they're getting the upstream project. A fork distributed under the same
name creates confusion about who maintains it, who is responsible for
security fixes, and whose roadmap it follows. The trademark policy
keeps that signal honest.

The license deliberately keeps software freedom (modify, fork, redistribute
within the noncommercial grant) separate from name rights (these stay
with the trademark owner). This is the same pattern used by the Linux
Foundation for "Linux", Mozilla for "Firefox" (which is why Debian
historically shipped "Iceweasel"), and the Apache Software Foundation
for project names.
